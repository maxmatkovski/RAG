The Roman Empire was one of the largest empires in ancient history. At its peak, it stretched from Britain in the northwest to Egypt in the southeast. It was founded in 27 BC when Augustus Caesar became the first emperor, ending the Roman Republic.

The fall of the Western Roman Empire occurred in 476 AD when the Germanic chieftain Odoacer deposed the last Roman emperor, Romulus Augustulus. Historians consider this a defining moment in the transition from antiquity to the Middle Ages.

The Colosseum in Rome was completed in 80 AD under Emperor Titus. It could hold between 50,000 and 80,000 spectators and was used for gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and public executions.

Julius Caesar was a Roman general and statesman who played a critical role in the transformation of the Roman Republic into the Roman Empire. He was assassinated on March 15, 44 BC — a date known as the Ides of March.

Roman roads were engineering marvels that connected the empire. The phrase "all roads lead to Rome" reflects how the road network radiated outward from the capital. These roads remained in use long after the empire fell.

The Roman Senate was a governing body that originated in the early days of Rome. During the Republic, it held great power over legislation and foreign policy. Under the emperors, its power diminished significantly.

Latin was the official language of the Roman Empire. It evolved into the Romance languages — including Italian, Spanish, French, Portuguese, and Romanian — which are spoken by hundreds of millions of people today.

Constantine the Great was the first Roman emperor to convert to Christianity, issuing the Edict of Milan in 313 AD, which granted religious tolerance throughout the empire. He also founded Constantinople, the city that would become the capital of the Eastern Roman Empire.

The Eastern Roman Empire, also known as the Byzantine Empire, survived the fall of the Western Empire and continued for nearly a thousand years, until Constantinople fell to the Ottoman Turks in 1453 AD.

Hadrian's Wall was built by Emperor Hadrian starting in 122 AD across northern Britain to guard the Roman province from raids by the Picts. It stretched 73 miles from coast to coast and is one of the best-preserved Roman monuments.
